About

I am a senior scientist of the Spanish National Research Council (CSIC), currently holding the position of Investigador Científico — the 2nd highest of the three CSIC permanent staff levels — a position I won through national competition in 2022.

My research focuses on understanding the origin and evolution of the universe through theory, simulations, and observational data, with particular emphasis on the Large-Scale Structure traced by galaxies and the Cosmic Microwave Background (CMB). I am a builder of major astronomical surveys including PLANCK, ARCHEOPS, DES, PAU, and Euclid, and a member of DESI and LSST.

Highlights from my research include pioneering the first detection of the ISW effect — the first physical evidence for dark energy independent of supernovae data — recognised by Science magazine as the #1 Breakthrough of the Year in 2003 across all fields of science. Since 2005 I have led the MICE simulation programme, one of the largest cosmological simulations ever produced, and contributed to developing CosmoHub, now serving 1,000+ research groups worldwide.

I have directed 5 doctoral theses and 7 Master theses, taught ~120 hours at the IFAE-ICE-UAB Master in High-Energy Physics, Astrophysics and Cosmology, and delivered 40+ invited talks internationally. My work has been covered by The New York Times, Science, and Scientific American.
